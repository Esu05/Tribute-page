Atal Bihari Vajpayee

A statesman of grace, whose words inspired, whose actions left a lasting trace.

Here's a time line of Atal Bihari Vajpayee

  1. Born:
    • December 25, 1924, in Gwalior, British India.
  2. Political Entry:
    • Vajpayee started his political career as a member of the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) in the 1940s.
    • He was a founding member of the Bharatiya Jana Sangh (BJS) in 1951.
  3. First Term as Prime Minister:
    • Vajpayee served as the Prime Minister of India for the first time from May 16 to June 1, 1996.
  4. Second Term as Prime Minister:
    • He became the Prime Minister again on March 19, 1998, leading the National Democratic Alliance (NDA) government.
    • Under his leadership, India conducted a series of nuclear tests on May 11 and 13, 1998, in Pokhran.
  5. Kargil War:
    • Vajpayee led the country during the Kargil War with Pakistan in 1999.
  6. Agra Summit:
    • In July 2001, Vajpayee hosted the Agra Summit with the then Pakistan President, Pervez Musharraf, in an attempt to resolve the long-standing issues between India and Pakistan.
  7. Third Term as Prime Minister:
    • Vajpayee's third term as Prime Minister began in October 1999 and continued until May 22, 2004.
  8. Golden Quadrilateral Project:
    • Vajpayee launched the ambitious Golden Quadrilateral highway project to connect major cities across India.
  9. Bus Diplomacy:
    • Vajpayee took a historic bus journey to Lahore, Pakistan, in February 1999, to improve diplomatic relations between the two countries.
  10. Poetry and Oratory:
    • Apart from his political career, Vajpayee was known for his eloquent speeches and poetry. He was a gifted orator and writer.
  11. Health Issues:
    • Vajpayee's health deteriorated in the later years of his life, and he withdrew from active politics.
  12. Death:
    • Atal Bihari Vajpayee passed away on August 16, 2018, at the age of 93.

Atal Bihari Vajpayee is remembered as a statesman, a skilled politician, and a key figure in Indian politics, known for his contributions to national development and foreign policy initiatives.
